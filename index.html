About Me

A born storyteller

Stories have always been facinating. The way they can captivate an audience, the meaning behind them, to the way they can tell a narrative of a group of people. That's where it all started really. I was born to become an anthropologist. Learning about the stories of people, and having the great honor and responsibility to tell their stories. I continued my journey from anthropologist to IT specialist.... wait? How did that happen? Oh right, 2020 and the pandemic. Struggling to find a job in my field right as the pandemic had occured, I transistioned to a different field that I had some expertise in, but now that the world is beginning to heal, I find it time that I try to go back to my roots. Becoming a UX/UI Designer is how I feel that I can cleave both fields. Being able to help people feel good while using technology, a tool so important to us modern homo sapiens.

Work

Cairn

My first solo app

My first project in my UX bootcamp, Cairn was an idea that I came up with and designed from the ground up. The basic premise was to create a travel app, and digging into my roots as an anthropologist, I wanted to create an app that was community based. Thinking about how cairns are created by other travellers as a way to guide others, I named my app thus to retain that spirit of community helping and advice.

Buffer

Group project: Lead Researcher

Buffer was my first group project. While working on this app, I took on the role of user researcher but also at times I would be a sort of team leader/organizer. Helping our team meet certain deadlines while also trying to make sure that everyone withing the project felt that their ideas were heard and expressed. Never forgetting that the end goal was to create an app for our users, not for us kept most decesions in check.

drawchange

Researcher/designer

My first non-profit work was heavily influenced by design. The nonprofit website, drawgchange, was alreayd a well desing website in terms of art and color, but upon further scrutiny we realized from users we interviewed that it required a lot more work in terms of volunteering and donating. By simplyfing the work needed to volunteer and donate, our hope was that more users in the future would be more inclined to do both, and therefore give more resources to the organization that will further help the community it serves.
